About

Fueled by the effects of climate change, poor forest health, and increasing populations, wildfires are becoming more frequent and more destructive, costing more money and placing more people at risk. Being able to quantify the intensity of past fire activity is an important component of fire prevention and education, planning, mitigation, and science activities designed to reduce the negative effects of fire. In recent years, many countries and agencies have begun to make past fire occurrence data available online to aid in these efforts. Unfortunately, this data lacks much standardization, may be hard to obtain, and storage formats may vary from text files to databases to GIS shapefiles.

We do the work to make this data usable by standardizing required fields to ISO formats (country, states, dates), outputting a simple JSON response structure, generating a browsable API for human-readability, and adding some extra query functionality to make searching records a bit easier. All records are required to have at least a start date and some spatial context (either latitude/longitude or state/province), however, most records have a final fire size, fire out date, fire name, and cause as well.

The API should make consuming these records in your analyses, visualizations, and web and mobile applications near effortless. Ready to get started? Try an example or take a look at the documentation for more info.
