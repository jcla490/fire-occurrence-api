About

Fueled by the effects of climate change, poor forest health, and increasing populations, wildfires are becoming more frequent and more destructive, costing more money and placing more people at risk. Being able to quantify the intensity of past fire activity and risk is an important component of fire prevention and education, planning, mitigation, and science activities designed to reduce the negative effects of fire. In recent years, many countries and agencies have begun to make past fire occurrence data available online. Unfortunately, this data lacks much standardization, may not be easily obtained, and formats may vary from text files to databases to GIS shapefiles.

We do the work to standardize this data by providing a simple JSON response structure, a browsable API for human-readability, and some value-added parameters to make searching records a bit easier. We focus on keeping the data that matters most: fire start date, final size, cause, latitude, and longitude while keeping optional fields like fire name and fire out date when they are provided by the source system.

The API should make consuming these records in your analyses, visualizations, and web and mobile applications near effortless. Ready to get started? Try an example or take a look at the documentation for more info.
